Chapter 03 · Doctor Experience

Trust begins before the first appointment.

Usually all you get is a name and a wall of qualifications. But on the way to the appointment, the questions you actually carry are quieter: Will this person listen? Will they rush me? Will I have to explain everything twice and still feel unheard? So those are the ones we answer first.

Credentials tell you they're capable.
Connection tells you you're in good hands.

Both matter. But only one of them calms the quiet worry a person carries into the room.

In their words
“My first job isn't to examine you. It's to make sure you don't have to repeat your story for the fifth time and still feel like the eleventh chart of the morning. Everything good in medicine starts the moment you stop bracing yourself.”
Dr. Elena Marchetti
Internal Medicine · 16 years of practice

Before your visit, you'll understand how Elena approaches care, the conditions she sees most often, and exactly what your first conversation will feel like — unhurried, plain-spoken and entirely about you.
